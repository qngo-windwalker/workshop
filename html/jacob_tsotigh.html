Jacob Tsotigh

is a Technical Assistance Coordinator for the South Central Regional Comprehensive Center at the University of Oklahoma and former Indian Education Director for Anadarko Public Schools. He previously worked with American Indian Research & Development’s Indian Technical Assistance Center Five which was funded by the USDE’s Office of Indian Education. He has worked for over 25 years in Indian education as an administrator, technical assistance provider, and advocate. Tsotigh received his Bachelor of Arts in History from the University of Oklahoma and his Masters in Education in Gifted and Talented from Oklahoma City University and a Masters in Education in Educational Administration from the University of Oklahoma. Tsotigh is a member of the Kiowa Tribe of Oklahoma and a past president of the Oklahoma Council for Indian Education. He is married to Alana Quetone, a classroom teacher in the Mid-Del School District. They have been married for 31 years and have four children, one deceased, and two grandsons. He has been involved with Indian education issues for over 25 years and is an advocate for Indian people and their issues in general. He was recognized by the Oklahoma Indian Times in 1999 as the Second Favorite Indian Role Model of the Present in their annual reader’s poll, an honor of which he is most proud.
